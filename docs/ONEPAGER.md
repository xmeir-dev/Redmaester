# Redmaester

**Turn your bookmark graveyard into fuel for your AI agents.**

---

## The Problem

People bookmark 10x more than they'll ever read. On X (Twitter) alone, power users accumulate thousands of bookmarks — insights, threads, tutorials, market signals — that vanish into a broken UI with no search, no filters, and no organization.

But the real problem isn't the UI. It's that even a perfect bookmark manager doesn't solve the core issue: **you have more saved knowledge than you have time to process.** And in the age of AI agents, that unprocessed knowledge is wasted leverage.

Your bookmarks are a solar panel collecting sunlight. Without a harvester, that energy just dissipates.

---

## The Solution

Redmaester is an AI-powered knowledge harvesting platform that transforms your bookmarks into structured, actionable intelligence — and feeds it directly to your AI agents.

**How it works:**

1. **Sync** — Pulls bookmarks from X (Twitter), with LinkedIn and other sources coming next
2. **Classify** — AI automatically categorizes each bookmark into topic domains (no manual tagging)
3. **Extract** — Generates structured micro-skills: tactical, actionable knowledge distilled from each bookmark
4. **Synthesize** — Builds living master knowledge bases per topic, continuously updated as new bookmarks arrive
5. **Deliver** — Feeds structured knowledge to your AI agents via CLI and API, making them smarter at the things you care about

---

## Who It's For

**AI power users** — founders, builders, and professionals who already use AI agents in their workflow and want to give those agents better context from the content they consume daily.

These are people who:
- Bookmark 20+ posts a week and read maybe 5
- Use AI agents (Claude, GPT, custom) for research, writing, and decision-making
- Want their agents to know what they know — without manually copying context every time

## What Makes Redmaester Different

| Feature | Traditional Bookmark Tools | Redmaester |
|---|---|---|
| Organization | Manual folders & tags | AI auto-classification |
| Output | A list you scroll through | Structured knowledge artifacts |
| Purpose | Save for *you* to read later | Harvest for *your agents* to use now |
| Value over time | Decays (more saves = more noise) | Compounds (more saves = smarter agents) |

Redmaester isn't a better bookmark manager. It's a **knowledge compiler** — turning raw saved content into structured intelligence that compounds over time.
